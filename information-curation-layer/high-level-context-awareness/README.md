Introduction</h1>

<p>In Mining Minds the sensory data is modelled ontologically in the form of RDF Triples which are fed to HLCA for reasoning purpose.  The underlying Mining Minds Context Ontology (MMCO) models context for human behavior identification in order to enable the provision of personalized health and wellness services in Mining Minds. In Mining Minds, human context is understood as any information characterizing the physical, mental and social situation of a person which enables the identification of human behavior.  Furthermore, human context is here categorized into two different levels of abstraction: low-level context (LLC) and high-level context (HLC).  HLC has further two main classes, Physical Activity high level context (PA-HLC) and nutrition high level context (N-HLC).  PA-HLC comprises of activities, locations and emotions include daily contexts like office work, commuting, house work, gardening, amusement, exercising, sleeping, and inactivity whereas N-HLC consists of carbohydrates, protein and fats determined as per rules from activity eating, location home or restaurant, with several emotions and having any of the food items from the list of 57 identified food under 10 broader categories.<br>

<h2><a id="user-content-small_red_triangle_down-intent--applicability-for-bridge-design-pattern" class="anchor" href="#small_red_triangle_down-intent--applicability-for-bridge-design-pattern" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a><g-emoji alias="small_red_triangle_down" fallback-src="https://assets-cdn.github.com/images/icons/emoji/unicode/1f53b.png" ios-version="6.0">ðŸ”»</g-emoji> Intent &amp; Applicability for Bridge Design Pattern</h2>

<p>In the Bridge design pattern we decouple an abstraction from its implementation so that the two can vary independently.  </p>

<ul>
<li>We avoided permanent binding between an abstraction and its implementation.</li>
<li>Both the abstractions and their implementations are extensible by subclassing. This implementation of Bridge pattern allows to  combine the different abstractions and implementations and extend them independently</li>
<li>changes in the implementation of an abstraction has no impact on clients</li>
<li>Shares an implementation among multiple objects and fact remains hidden from the client. </li>
<li>We implemented Bridge design pattern for <strong>Context</strong> Class as an abstraction in the <strong>mm.icl.hlc.OntologyTools</strong> package.  <strong>NutritionContext</strong> and <strong>PhysicalActivityContext</strong> Classes work independently and are extended from <strong>Context</strong> Class.<br></li>
<li>Similarly <strong>ContextHandler</strong> class is extended from <strong>AbstractHandler</strong> class in the <strong>mm.icl.hlc.ContextOntologyManager</strong>, which can be extended for future use.<br></li>
<li>The features of Bridge design patterns were also used in <strong>ContextOntology</strong> class which was extended from <strong>AbstractOntology</strong> implemented in  <strong>mm.icl.hlc.OntologyTools</strong> package.</li>
<li>Lastly High level context reasoner i.e. <strong>HLCReasoner</strong> class is extended from <strong>AbstractReasoner</strong> class in the <strong>mm.icl.hlc.HLCReasoner</strong> package.<br>
These were the core areas of High Level Context Awareness in which Bridge Design Patterns were used.</li>
</ul>

<h2><a id="user-content-small_red_triangle_down-intent--applicability-for-singleton-design-pattern" class="anchor" href="#small_red_triangle_down-intent--applicability-for-singleton-design-pattern" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a><g-emoji alias="small_red_triangle_down" fallback-src="https://assets-cdn.github.com/images/icons/emoji/unicode/1f53b.png" ios-version="6.0">ðŸ”»</g-emoji> Intent &amp; Applicability for Singleton Design Pattern</h2>

<p>Ensured that a class only has one instance, and provide a global point of access to it.  We also used singleton design pattern so that </p>

<ul>
<li>the instance is only extensible by subclassing without modifying their code.</li>
<li>there must be exactly one instance of a class, and it must be accessible and enforced to clients through authorized access point</li>
<li>Singleton Design pattern in this implementation was applied for <strong>ContextQueryGenerator</strong> and <strong>ContextHandler</strong> classes so that they can be used further without modifying codes.</li>
</ul>

<li>  This work has presented an ontology-based method for deriving PA-HLC and N-HLC out of the combination of cross-domain low-level context primitives, namely activities, locations, emotions and food. </li>
<li>  The modeling of the low-level, PA-HLC and N-HLC are defined through the so-called Mining Minds Context Ontology. </li>
<li>  The processing and inference of contexts is performed by the Mining Minds High-Level Context Architecture implementation.</li>

<li>  The Mining Minds Context Ontology has also been designed to procure the identification PA-HLC and N-HLC even in the absence of emotion and Food information. </li>
